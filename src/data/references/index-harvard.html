Where Tradition Comes to Life

As the oldest university in America, life at Harvard is steeped in rich traditions that connect generations of students and create lasting memories.

Hasty Pudding

Each year, performers who have made lasting contributions to the arts are chosen as Hasty Pudding Man and Woman of the Year and are awarded a coveted pudding pot.

Learn about Hasty Pudding's star-studded history

Housing Day

A special day every spring where first-year students find out which of the 12 Harvard Houses they will call home for the remainder of their undergraduate years.

Explore Harvard's Housing Day

Harvard Arts Festival

Formerly the Harvard ARTS FIRST Festival, this annual campus-wide event highlights and celebrates all forms of creativity across the university community.

Learn more about this year's festival

Commencement

Dating back to 1642, Commencement at Harvard celebrates the achievements of our exceptional degree candidates and those who supported them on their journeys.

Learn more about this year's ceremony

Move-in Day

An emotional day for incoming students and their families, move-in day marks the beginning of a new journey filled with discovery and growth.

Read about this pivotal day

Harvard-Yale Game

Each year, Harvard and Yale vie for bragging rights in a football rivalry dating back to 1875, known simply as "The Game" to generations of alumni and students.

Find out more about "The Game"

Financial Aid at Harvard

Harvard is committed to making education accessible to talented students from all backgrounds. Our generous financial aid programs ensure that a Harvard education is within reach for families across the income spectrum.

Expanding Financial Aid

Harvard College is free for students from families with annual incomes of $100,000 or less and tuition-free for students from families with annual incomes of $200,000 or less.

Learn more about Harvard College financial aid
